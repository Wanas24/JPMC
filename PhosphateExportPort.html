Phosphate export port

  2. Phosphate export port

Why are we your choice?

The phosphate export port was established in its new location in 2012 on the Red Sea coast, 20 km away. South of the city of Aqaba to carry out the task of mixing the types of phosphate coming from the mines.

The new phosphate port meets the specifications required by customers and is loaded onto ships heading to countries around the world, with an export card of up to 7 million tons per year. The total area of ​​the export port is (138,500) square meters, as it consists of a truck unloading area and warehouses. The storage area is (127,000) square meters, and the marine pier is (11,500) square metres.

Truck unloading area and storage warehouses

It consists of 12 unloading stations for trucks and the unloading rate reaches (20-30) One thousand tons per day.

The unloaded phosphate is transported via conveyor belts and stored in 6 warehouses used for various facilities Types of phosphate, with a total storage capacity of (240) thousand tons.
A protection system is available to separate mineral impurities from phosphate during unloading into warehouses During loading on ships (Magnetic Separator).

Pier area

The quay area is (250) meters long and is used to secure safe berthing for ships of various sizes from (5000 - 100000) tons.

The marine quay area contains facilities for loading ships with phosphate (ship loaders), with a loading capacity of (2200) tons/hour, and a pipe conveyor system for phosphate from the warehouse area to the loading point on the ship with a length of (1600) meters.
The conveyor belts are equipped with an automatic sampling system prior to loading onto the ship.
To obtain representative samples of the loaded shipments.
The port's operating system for unloading and loading operations is based on the global SCADA system.

Preservation of the causative agents of the occupational virus

Based on the company's vision of preserving the environment, the port is designated for exporting phosphate Great attention has been paid to this matter through several measures as follows:

Samples are taken from mines and phosphates loaded on ships, and the results of the analyzes are monitored on an ongoing basis A department specialized in quality control, and based on the results of the analysis, determines the mixing ratios of the types to achieve Customer requirements.
  • Reducing dust emission during unloading trucks, loading into warehouses and loading onto the ship (De-dusting System)Through control systems that undergo a regular preventive maintenance program.
  • Recycling water by treating it according to standards set by the state and using it to irrigate crops.
  • Providing firefighting systems distributed in all locations, according to the requirements of the Jordanian Civil Defense and international codes.
  • Continuous monitoring of workers’ commitment to using public safety tools and following all instructions related to occupational safety.
  • Holding internal courses and courses with external parties to raise awareness of risks and their prevention.
Several external parties conduct environmental checks, such as the level of dust in the air and water specifications, to monitor compliance The port meets the required standards, as well as periodic medical examinations for workers to ensure their safety.
In this regard, the phosphate export port obtained certificates from the International Organization for Standardization SO 9001, ISO 18001, ISO 45001

This conf irms the port’s commitment to achieving all quality, environmental and safety standards
