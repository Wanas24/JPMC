Planning and strategic thinking

Strategic planning and thinking are the most important factors for the continued success of Jordan Phosphate Mines Company.

  • Establishing a factory to produce aluminum fluoride with a production capacity of 25 thousand tons annually to use the FSA material produced by IJC & JIFCO in partnership between the Jordan Phosphate Mines Company and the Indian Alufloride Company at a cost estimated at about 30 million US dollars. The company has been registered and production from this project is expected to start during the last quarter of 2024.
  • Phosphate washing water treatment project in Al-Shidiya mines with the local company Engicon to treat 9,000 cubic meters per day and recycle it, on the BOT principle. This project is expected to be completed at the end of 2024. The project cost is estimated at about 10 million US dollars.
  • Phosphate flotation project with the Ideal Company and designed by the American Jacob Company with a production capacity of (2) million tons annually, by exploiting the stored phosphate refuse, and on the BOT principle. Civil construction work for the project has begun and it is expected to be completed by the end of the third quarter of the year 2024. The project cost is estimated at about 85 million US dollars.
  • The greening of the gypsum mountain in the industrial complex in Aqaba has begun with the staff of the Jordan Phosphate Mines Company and a joint study between the Jordan Phosphate Mines Company and the Aqaba Special Economic Zone Authority (ASEZA) as an optimal solution to get rid of this environmental impact in line with the vision of (ASEZA) to create recreational sites and green spaces to constitute a qualitative addition to tourism in Aqaba. With the efforts of the Aqaba Special Economic Zone Authority, the FAO organization joined this project in order to completely green the gypsum mountain on scientific foundations based on specialized research. It is expected that this project will be completed within the next three years. An amount of one million Jordanian dinars has been allocated for this purpose.
  • The company has started the technical studies to convert the ammonia tanks in the industrial complex / Aqaba to (Double wall and Double Integrity). It is expected that the design and implementation works for the tanks will take about 18 months from the date of completion of the current works of Dar Al-Handasah Company. The cost of constructing one tank is estimated at about 35 million US dollars. The Water Dome is currently being designed for the current ammonia tanks and according to the highest safety standards to deal with emergency cases.
