Diammonium Phosphate

  1. Home
  2. Diammonium Phosphate

identification:

It is an inorganic compound and a salt of phosphoric acid and is a phosphate Organophosphates are esters of phosphoric acid. And longer Phosphate is an underground resource and is extracted to use phosphorus In several agricultural and industrial uses.

Manufacturing method

Ammonia is imported from several Arab and foreign countries via ships. Where ammonia is pumped directly from the ship

It is pumped using a 14-inch diameter pipe into two tanks, the capacity of the first tank being (30,000) tons The second has a capacity of (10,000) tons, and the temperature of ammonia is maintained inside the tanks Up to (33) degrees Celsius below zero using special compressors.
Ammonia is pumped to the reactor, where it reacts with concentrated phosphoric acid and the resulting liquid mixture (SLURRY) is pumped to the fertilizer granulation device, where it is mixed with the returned fertilizer, and another amount of ammonia is added to it to obtain the required mixture ratio from the granulation device. The fertilizer is transferred to the dryer, which is a rotating device where the fertilizer is dried with hot combustion gases resulting from burning fuel oil so that the moisture content does not exceed (%). The fertilizer is then transferred to sieves to isolate the required sized granules where they are cooled in a dryer-like cooling device and sent to storage rooms.

Production capacity

Diammonium phosphate fertilizer is produced with a production capacity of 2250 tons per unit per day.

The concentrated phosphoric acid produced in the industrial complex is used, in addition to imported ammonia, to produce fertilizer, which is stored in special warehouses with a capacity of 60,000 tons and sold in the local market and international markets such as India, Ethiopia, Turkey, Iraq, and others.
The produced fertilizer contains (18%) nitrogen and (46%) phosphorus pentoxide, which is characterized by being granular and easily soluble, which leads to its easy use by the plant and enables us to use it using agricultural machines. It is used for all crops and trees, whether in rainfed or irrigated areas.

Uses:

Fertilizer is used in agriculture either directly or to produce other types of fertilizers such as liquid, suspended and compound fertilizers.
